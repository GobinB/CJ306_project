Key Supreme Court Decisions

2001

Kyllo v. United States

Thermal imaging of homes constitutes a search requiring a warrant

Key Points:
  • Use of thermal imaging device to detect heat patterns in home
  • Court ruled 5-4 that this constitutes a search under Fourth Amendment
  • Established principle that advanced technology can't bypass Fourth Amendment protections
  • Impact: Set precedent for protecting privacy against advancing surveillance technology
2012

United States v. Jones

GPS tracking device installation constitutes a search

Key Points:
  • 28-day GPS surveillance of vehicle without warrant
  • Unanimous decision finding Fourth Amendment violation
  • Introduced "mosaic theory" of privacy
  • Impact: Limited long-term electronic surveillance without warrant
2014

Riley v. California

Warrantless cell phone searches incident to arrest are unconstitutional

Key Points:
  • Unanimous decision protecting cell phone privacy
  • Distinguished cell phones from other physical items
  • Recognized vast amount of personal data on modern phones
  • Impact: Major victory for digital privacy rights
2018

Carpenter v. United States

Government must obtain warrant for cell-site location information

Key Points:
  • 127 days of cell phone location data obtained without warrant
  • Modified third-party doctrine for digital age
  • Recognized expectation of privacy in physical movements
  • Impact: Landmark decision for digital privacy rights
